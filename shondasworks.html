Shonda's works

By Maíra Araújo

As you might have noticed, I am here to talk about Shonda Rhimes’s best works in my opinion.
Both Grey’s Anatomy and How to get away with murder are extremely gripping series. They are loaded with drama and the thing I think I like the most is that we can find all kind of people represented in them. As an LGBTQI+ person, I like to see this kind of representation in series as it looks more like real life for me.

Grey’s Anatomy is a medical series, almost 90% based in hospitals and the other 10% is the life of the doctors out of it. There is a lot of drama, a lot of deaths (important deaths) that make me feel like I know the person who is dying. It is absolutely engaging.
For Rhimes to write something so specific about one kind of profession, she might have to study and understand a lot of things about this profession, as in the series we can find loads of diagnosis, surgeries, symptoms, etc.

How to get away with murder, is not as dramatic as Grey’s Anatomy but still, there is a lot of drama. For me this series is more about mystery, about hiding your flaws.
This series is about a famous and powerful lawyer that teaches in a University and some recruited students. After being recruited by her, all their lives change a lot as they commit crimes, try to hide it, find out a lot about the life of their teacher and sometimes they even go crazy. There are lies, love, fake love, tears and, specially, the rise of flaws in apparently flawless people, just like real life.
